More info about Tagaytay

Tagaytay is a second class city and was founded in June 21, 1938, it has 34 Barangays which has a population of 71,181 and 16,095 households (based on 2015 census).

Fun fact!

“Tagâ, Itáy!”

Fun fact, A legend is behind the name Tagaytay. The story involved a boy and his father who went out to hunt wild boars one day. They chased a boar, which then turned to attack them, starting with the father. The son shouted, “Tagâ, Itáy!” The boy shouted repeatedly and loudly that it echoed in the alleys of the ridge. Another story, and a more feasible one relating to the origin of the place, is naming the place after Tagaytay, which is a Tagalog term for a ridge. A ridge refers to a chain of hills or mountains forming a continued elevated crest that can be seen from a distance.

Due to its breathtaking surroundings, Tagaytay is one of the southern Philippines' most popular destinations. It even has its own nickname which is "The Second Summer Capital of the Philippines" the first being Baguio. It has a cool climate due to its high altitude and views of Taal Lake and the Taal Volcano. This allows you multiple vantage locations from which to see both the volcano and lake. Additionally, this explains why tourism is one of its main industries.

Tagaytay City has a tropical monsoon classification according to the Köppen scale. In comparison to other cities in Metro Manila and the rest of the Philippines, the city boasts milder temperatures and lesser humidity. It tends to get colder from December to February, the average temperature is 22°C. Compared to Metro Manila's 81% humidity level, Tagaytay's humidity level is 78%. Tagaytay City can also occasionally be foggy because to its high elevation.
